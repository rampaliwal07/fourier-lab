Theory of Fourier Series

Mathematical foundation

A Fourier series represents a periodic function f(x) with period 2L as:

f(x) = a₀/2 + Σₙ₌₁^∞ [ aₙ cos(nπx/L) + bₙ sin(nπx/L) ]

Coefficients are given by:
a₀ = (1/L) ∫₋ᴸᴸ f(x) dx
aₙ = (1/L) ∫₋ᴸᴸ f(x) cos(nπx/L) dx
bₙ = (1/L) ∫₋ᴸᴸ f(x) sin(nπx/L) dx

Key properties: Even functions → only cosine terms (bₙ=0). Odd functions → only sine terms (aₙ=0).

Gibbs phenomenon: Overshoot near discontinuities.
